Project 1: U.S Census Data Analysis and Visualization using R

Cleaned the 10,000+ rows of U.S census data by removing missing and corrupt values, and accurately named the columns. Analyzed and visualized the data to understand the economic disadvantage of individuals not born in the U.S., the ethnic composition of the population earning less than $50k/year, the impact of gender and age on poverty, and the distribution of professions within the population segment. Identified that U.S. born White men with a maximum education level of a high school diploma and married to a civilian spouse were at the highest risk of poverty. This information can be used to recommend educational initiatives to encourage college education among the White male population and to design schemes to support men with civilian spouses, particularly if the male partner is White.

Project 2: Customer Churn Prediction for a Bank using Python

Conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA) to gain insights into the distribution of features and visualize patterns related to churned customers in a bank, such as the country with the highest churn, gender differences in churn, age and credit score of churned customers in country with highest churn. Proceeded to find salary range, account balance, tenure and activity of customers from country with the highrst churn. Observed that Germany has the highest churn among all countries that the bank operates in. Most churned German customers earned less than $50,000/annum, were between ages 40-50, had a Fair FICO credit score, and had spent <=2 years with the bank. This information can be used by the bank to make customer retention strategies. I further proceeded to make a predictive model that would tell us which exact customers of the bank are going to churn. For this, I used Correlation based Feature Selection for selecting the features from the pre-cleaned data that would be most useful to the model in-order to predict churn. Next, I created a preprocessor pipeline, splitting the data into training and testing sets, and fitting a logistic regression model and a random forest model to make predictions on customer churn.

Project 3:Sentiment Analysis on Amazon Product Reviews Using Apache Spark: A Large-Scale Analysis

In this project, PySpark was used to perform sentiment analysis on 200,000+ Amazon reviews from two product categories, using its Machine Learning Library (MLliB). Preprocessing and machine learning text feature extraction were used for improved sentiment analysis classification outcomes. The project concluded with a performance evaluation of the classification algorithms used such as Naive Bayes, Random Forest and Logistic Regression for the sentiment analysis

Project 4: Country Segmentaion for NGO funding using Python

Analysed the country data of an NGO to categorise countries using some socio-economic and health factors that determine the overall development of the country. This information is used by the NGO to determine the countries that need immediate help. I conducted exploratory data analysis, treated outliers, calculated descriptive statistics, and checked cluster tendency using the Hopkins statistic for the country dataset. Performed data scaling using StandardScaler.
